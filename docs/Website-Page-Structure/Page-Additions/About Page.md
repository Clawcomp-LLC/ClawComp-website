## **These phrases fade in with epic animations upon landing on the page: “Free Mac minis, $17,500 in prizes, an Openclaw community, Networking with Link Ventures, VC investment opportunities, Get flown out to Boston with \- All expenses paid, access to exclusive Alex Finn content/advice”**

**ClawComp** is a three-month build program — not a weekend hackathon – targeted at bringing together an elite community of Openclaw innovators. It is designed to fit around what you are already building. Whether you’re a startup founder, simply building an app with friends, experimenting with AI tools, or just brainstorming business ideas, ClawComp challenges you to integrate OpenClaw into that work and push it further than you would on your own. Clawcomp rewards teams who build real Openclaw automations with measurable outcomes, not wrapper products that fall apart under scrutiny.

**Local Hardware.** Every accepted team receives a Mac Mini funded by Link Ventures. You keep it regardless of where you finish. We were sick of seeing students not building with Openclaw because of these upfront costs. After using Openclaw on a Mac mini for 100+ hours in the span of 4 weeks, we knew we had to provide this opportunity to our classmates.

**The Build Period.** Teams of one to three members have approximately three months to design, build, and iterate on their autonomous AI systems. Throughout the program, teams submit short weekly video updates — high-energy 60-second clips that document their process, show what they built, and demonstrate their ability to sell what they are working on. These are review by the Clawcomp team and serve as both a progress record and an evaluation tool. Teams that opt in will have their content featured across Link Ventures' media channels \- Promptcast, and partner creator networks.  
**Events.** The program kicks off with a launch webinar and, for teams near Boston, an in-person pickup event at Link Studios in Cambridge where you can meet the team, connect with founders in the Link ecosystem, and pick up your hardware. Periodic online check-ins and community touchpoints keep teams connected throughout the build period.

**ClawWeek.** The competition culminates in a final summit week at Link Studios in Cambridge, Massachusetts from June 15 to June 18th. Teams are flown in to meet each other in person and push their projects to the finish line in hackathon style. All travel and housing expenses are taken care of by Link Ventures. Founders and investors from the Link ecosystem and beyond will be on site, as well as professors and researchers from Harvard, M.I.T. and more — Link portfolio companies will stop by the first floor to meet teams, ask questions, and evaluate pitches in real time. A live leaderboard tracks scores throughout the week, creating a competitive, high-energy environment as teams make their final push. The week closes with a formal presentation and award ceremony.  
**Prizes.** The top 3 teams receive cash prizes. The overall winner receives additional recognition and a set of high-value networking opportunities within the Link and venture community — including potential introductions, pitch opportunities, and access to events in the broader Link ecosystem. Every team walks away with their hardware and a documented record of what they built.  
**Who judges you?** Scoring is based on the quality of what you built, your ability to pitch it to other founders/the audience, and the strength of your online presence on Talvy. The judging panel draws from the Link community — founders, investors, and the organizing team — with input from the broader network of mentors and partners present during the final week. The best teams are not just technically impressive. They can also communicate clearly, iterate quickly, and demonstrate that what they built has real-world value.
